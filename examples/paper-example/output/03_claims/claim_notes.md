# Claim notes — green-space-wellbeing (FICTIONAL EXAMPLE)

## Extraction decisions

- **C02 typed as `observational_association`, not causal.** The design
  is a panel with individual fixed effects — stronger than
  cross-sectional, but the authors themselves state "estimates should
  not be interpreted as causal effects". The authors' own boundary is
  the ceiling; we do not exceed it even though FE models are sometimes
  marketed as "causal".
- **C03 confidence = low.** The subgroup finding appears in one
  abstract sentence with no effect size or interaction test reported.
  Full manuscript not provided. human_approval_required = yes, and the
  campaign brief carries the open question of whether to use it at all.
- **CI included in original_evidence but not in normalized_claim** —
  the 0.14 SD point estimate plus "modest" qualification is what
  content may carry; anyone needing the CI links to the paper.

## Deliberately excluded from communication

- The correspondence email (personal data; readers reach the author
  via the working-paper page instead).
- Any implication about policy ("cities should build more parks") —
  no ledger claim supports an intervention effect.

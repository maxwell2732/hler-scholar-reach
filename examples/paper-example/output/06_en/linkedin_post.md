# LinkedIn post (draft, FICTIONAL EXAMPLE)

Does living near green space go together with feeling better? We now
have longitudinal evidence — with clear boundaries on what it can say.

In a new working paper (not yet peer-reviewed), we follow 4,200 adults
in 12 districts across three survey waves (2018–2024). In individual
fixed-effects models, an increase in green-space access was associated
with higher self-reported well-being (0.14 SD).

Two things this study does NOT show: it does not show that green space
*causes* better well-being (access is not randomly assigned, and
residential sorting is a plausible alternative explanation), and
well-being is self-reported.

Why it still matters: longitudinal within-person evidence on this
question is scarce, and the effect estimate comes with its
uncertainty stated rather than hidden.

Working paper, comments very welcome:
https://example.edu/working-papers/green-space-wellbeing
